Industrial Statistics Research Unit (ISRU)

Brief Details: 	See also http://www.ncl.ac.uk/isru/

ISRU is a self-funding group of Industrial Statisticians attached to
SMSE (School of Mechanical and Systems Engineering) University of
Newcastle upon Tyne, UK.

Our principle aim is to help people from a variety of fields to apply
statistical methods within business and Industry in as user-friendly
way as possible.  The fields that we cover include Manufacturing;
processes, new product R&D, engineering, business flows, waste
reduction, continuous improvement, process monitoring. We research
into the 'best' structure of Genetic Algorithms, especially those used
for complex scheduling problems.  We are involved in optimisation of
all kinds.

We have been involved in 11 EC funded projects under the 4th and 5th
Frameworks, three of these as co-ordinators.  We run the Thematic
Network pro-ENBIS.  

All our senior staff are Chartered.  We are the largest resource of
its type attached to a University, in Europe.  We have 10 Industrial
Statisticians including Professor Coleman with Visiting Professors
Tony Greenfield and Roland Caulcutt.  Our Director Dave Stewardson is
a former President of ENBIS (see http://www.enbis.org/)
